In some cases, cookies may be used to collect personal information, or information that becomes personal information when combined with other data.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites function properly, improve efficiency, and provide reporting and analytics information to site operators.

Cookies set by the website operator (in this case, The Nexora Project) are called "first-party cookies." Cookies set by parties other than the website operator are called "third-party cookies." Third-party cookies enable external features or functionality to be provided on or through the website, such as advertising, interactive content, and analytics. The entities that set third-party cookies can recognize your device both when it visits our Website and when it visits certain other websites.

Why We Use Cookies

We use first- and third-party cookies for the following purposes:

  • Essential operations: Some cookies are strictly necessary for the Website to function, such as maintaining your login session and applying your display preferences.
  • User experience: Functional cookies remember your settings and preferences so you do not need to reconfigure them on each visit.
  • Analytics: Performance cookies allow us to monitor traffic, identify popular features, and improve the Website.
  • Advertising: Third-party advertising cookies serve relevant ads and measure their effectiveness.

Other Tracking Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use similar technologies such as web beacons (also known as "tracking pixels" or "clear gifs"). These are small graphic files containing a unique identifier that allow us to recognize when someone has visited our Website or opened an email. Web beacons help us monitor traffic patterns, deliver and communicate with cookies, understand referral sources, and measure the effectiveness of communications.

Many of these technologies depend on cookies to function properly. If you disable cookies, their functionality may be impaired.

Local Storage and Similar Technologies

Our Website uses browser local storage (including localStorage and sessionStorage) to store user preferences, theme settings, and other functional data locally on your device. This data is not transmitted to our servers and is used solely to enhance your browsing experience. You can clear local storage through your browser's developer tools or settings.
